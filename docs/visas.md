## Detailed Country Guides

### France & Spain (Schengen Zone)
*   **US State Dept Status**: Level 2 (Exercise Increased Caution - Terrorism/Civil Unrest)
*   **Visa Requirement**: Visa-free for up to 90 days for tourism.
*   **Entry Rules**:
    *   Passport must be valid for at least **3 months** beyond your planned date of departure from the Schengen area.
    *   **Currency**: Must declare amounts over €10,000.
*   **Roadbook Advice**:
    *   Crit-Air stickers required for some French cities.
    *   Compulsory car kit: Warning triangles, reflective jackets (accessible from inside car), spare bulbs (Spain).
    *   Speed traps are common.

### Gibraltar (British Overseas Territory)
*   **Status**: Not part of Schengen.
*   **Visa Requirement**: **None** for US citizens for stays < 90 days.
*   **New Requirement (2025)**: **UK Electronic Travel Authorisation (ETA)**.
    *   Starting Jan 8, 2025, non-European nationals (including US citizens) will need an ETA to enter the UK and its territories.
    *   Cost: ~£10. Apply online before travel.
*   **Border Note**: Crossing from Spain to Gibraltar and back involves exiting and re-entering the Schengen zone. Ensure your passport is stamped correctly to avoid overstay issues in Schengen.

### Morocco
*   **US State Dept Status**: Level 2 (Exercise Increased Caution - Terrorism)
*   **Visa Requirement**: Visa-free for up to 90 days.
*   **Entry Rules**:
    *   Passport must have at least **6 months** validity.
    *   **Drones are strictly prohibited** and will be confiscated.
*   **Roadbook Advice**:
    *   **Border**: Tangier Med is the preferred entry point (easier customs).
    *   **Paperwork**: Keep your "CIN" number handy if you've visited before. You will receive a white temporary import card for the car (keep this safe!).
    *   **Money**: Dirham is closed currency (cannot export). Exchange at border.
    *   **Sensitivity**: Do NOT refer to "Western Sahara" on maps/conversation; use "Southern Provinces".

### Mauritania
*   **US State Dept Status**: Level 3 (Reconsider Travel - Crime/Terrorism)
*   **Visa Requirement**: **YES**. Roadbook strongly advises getting **E-Visa in advance**.
    *   *Note*: US State Dept mentions visas should be obtained before travel.
*   **Entry Rules**:
    *   **Yellow Fever Vaccination Certificate is REQUIRED**.
    *   Passport validity: 6 months.
    *   **Alcohol**: Strictly prohibited. Do not bring any.
*   **Roadbook Advice**:
    *   **Border Crossing**: Cross at "No Man's Land" (Guerguerat). Arrive early (border closes for lunch).
    *   **Fees**: Expect multiple fees (~55€ Visa, ~30€ Insurance, ~10€ Passavant, ~40€ Park fees). **Bring Euros cash**.
    *   **Safety**: Landmines exist near the border with Western Sahara - **Do not leave the tarmac/graded track**.

### Senegal
*   **US State Dept Status**: Level 1 (Exercise Normal Precautions)
*   **Visa Requirement**: None for US citizens for stays < 90 days.
*   **Entry Rules**:
    *   Passport validity: 6 months.
    *   **Yellow Fever**: Technically required if arriving from endemic zone (Mauritania is one).
*   **Roadbook Advice**:
    *   **Border**: Cross at **Diama** (National Park route). **AVOID ROSSO** (banditry/corruption).
    *   **Car Paperwork**: "Senegal 1 Car Pass" (~170€) strongly recommended to simplify the "7-year-old car" import rule.
    *   **Driving**: Beware of massive speed bumps.

### The Gambia
*   **US State Dept Status**: Level 2 (Exercise Increased Caution - Health/Crime)
*   **Visa Requirement**: **YES**.
    *   Roadbook suggests VOA (Visa On Arrival) might be possible but risky/expensive. US State Dept lists fee as ~$108 for VOA or $200 via Embassy.
    *   *Recommendation*: Check current status closer to departure or get in advance if possible.
*   **Entry Rules**:
    *   Passport valid for duration of stay.
*   **Roadbook Advice**:
    *   **Border**: Karang or Farafeni bridge.
    *   **Ferries**: Barra ferry is unreliable; Farafeni bridge is the alternative (longer route).
    *   **Car Disposal**: Strict rules on donating cars. Must be done via approved charity (Rotary Club) to avoid customs fines.
