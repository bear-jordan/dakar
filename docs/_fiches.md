# The "Fiche" (Personal Information Sheet)

## What is a Fiche?
"Fiches" (pronounced *feesh*, French for "files") are single-page summaries of your passport and vehicle details. They are **critical** for travel in Morocco, Western Sahara, and Mauritania.

## Why do I need them?
Police checkpoints are frequent (sometimes every 20-50km). At each stop, officers must record your details manually.
*   **The Slow Way**: You hand over passports. The officer hand-copies details. You wait 15 minutes.
*   **The Fiche Way**: You hand over a pre-printed Fiche. The officer smiles, takes it, and waves you through. You wait 30 seconds.

## Action Plan
1.  **Create your Master Fiche**: Use the template below. Fill it in digitally.
2.  **Photos**: Add a small digital photo of yourself (optional but helpful).
3.  **Print**: Print **50-100 copies** per person.
    *   *Tip*: Print 4 to an A4 page and cut them up to save bulk.
4.  **Distribution**: Keep a stack on the dashboard ready to hand out through the window.
